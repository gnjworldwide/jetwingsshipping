Parboiled rice, also known as converted rice, is rice that has been partially boiled before milling. This process involves soaking, steaming, and drying the rice in its husk before removing the outer shell. The main purpose of parboiling is to improve the nutritional value of the rice and make it less prone to breaking during milling.
